The Web Cookie Initiative is a project by IT students, majoring in Network Systems. It is an initiative to spread cybersecurity awareness among internet users, starting with web cookies.

We've all seen that pop-up asking if we're okay with cookies on websites, right? Most sites won't whow you stuff unless you say "yes". Usually, we just click "yes" without really thinking about it. Understanding every cookie can be tricky, but this initiative will help us learn how web cookies work.

About Us

The people behind the initiative are 4th year college students of Mindanao State University-Iligan Institute of Technology (MSU-IIT), under the Bachelor of Science in Information Technology, Major in Network Systems. They are Rizza Shannyn Paredes, Abdul Karim Tawakim, and Michael Tecson. Along with the guidance of their capstone adviser, Prof. Erik Sala.

The three students have a passion for cybersecurity, gaming, graphic design, art, and web development. Together, they are on a mission to delete the concept of accepting “cookies” without knowing what it is for.

Where Are My Cookies? is a game under the Web Cookie Initiative. It is a pixel-art story-rich, role-playing game that aims to educate internet users about web cookies.

The team believes that through gamification, it will be a fun, interesting and effective way to learn about web cookies. The aims to simulate and capture the essence of how web cookies function, and how it affects a user’s internet experience, as well as the dangers it may bring.
